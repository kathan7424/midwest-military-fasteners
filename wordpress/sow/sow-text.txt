Project Background
White Label IQ will develop a B2B ecommerce website for Midwest Military Fasteners
http://www.mwmilitary.com/, a supplier of military-grade fasteners and hardware products. The
platform will provide existing customers with a fast and efficient way to search for products, place
orders, manage accounts, and access product-related documentation.
Unlike a traditional ecommerce website focused on product browsing, this solution will function as
a streamlined digital catalog and ordering system where customers can quickly locate products by
part number, review product information, and complete purchases with minimal steps. The
platform will also support customer-specific purchasing workflows, including quantity-based
pricing, tax-exempt accounts, compliance certifications, and approved Purchase Order (PO) / Net
30 payment options.

Project Objectives
The primary objectives of this project are to:
• Develop a modern B2B ecommerce platform that allows customers to quickly search for and
purchase military fasteners and related products online.
• Create a streamlined ordering experience focused on fast product discovery and efficient
purchasing workflows for existing business customers.
• Enable customers to search products by part number, product name, and other relevant
criteria to quickly locate required items.
• Support quantity-based pricing structures and customer-specific purchasing requirements.
• Provide approved customers with access to specialized purchasing options, including
Purchase Order (PO) and Net 30 payment methods.
• Implement tax-exempt account management, including document uploads, approval
workflows, and expiration tracking.
• Provide customers with access to product-related specifications and certifications
associated with their purchases.
• Improve operational efficiency by reducing manual processes related to order management,
customer documentation, and compliance-related requirements.
• Deliver a scalable platform capable of supporting future business growth, expanded product
catalogs, and additional functionality enhancements.

Scope of Work
Development
The development environment will be set up on Pantheon (client's provided), configured for a
headless WordPress/WooCommerce + Next.js architecture. WordPress serves exclusively as the
backend — managing the product catalog, orders, customer accounts, and WooCommerce
checkout logic. All customer-facing pages are built in Next.js (React), which consumes the
WooCommerce REST API. This replaces the WordPress theme entirely and delivers the fast,
performance-first experience required for MMF's industrial B2B audience.
Additionally, necessary plugins will be added to enhance the site's security and performance. This
will involve installing a security plugin to protect against malware and other potential threats, as
well as plugins to improve the site's speed, such as a caching plugin to enhance page load times.
CMS Pages Development:
Based on the project, here are the CMS-managed pages:
• Homepage
• About
• Quality
• Terms & Return Policy
• FAQs
• Contact Us
All remaining pages — including cart, checkout, and customer account pages — will use
WooCommerce's default page templates, styled to match the approved design. No custom
development will be done for these pages from scratch.

Features & Functionality
Product Catalog Management
• White Label IQ will configure and develop a centralized product catalog management
system that allows administrators to efficiently manage product information through the
website's administrative interface.
• The product catalog will support:
◦ Product categories and subcategories
◦ Product detail pages
◦ Product names and descriptions
◦ Product SKUs and part numbers
◦ Product pricing
◦ Inventory quantities
◦ Product specification documents and PDF attachments
• As part of the initial implementation, White Label IQ will import approximately 2,500
products using a client-provided spreadsheet. The catalog is expected to scale to 5,000–
10,000 products over time.
• The spreadsheet must contain all required product information, including product names,
descriptions, pricing, inventory levels, images, and supporting documentation where
applicable.
• A tooltip will be displayed on the package quantity column in the part series table, clarifying
that the quantity value represents the number of units per package, not the available
inventory count.
Product Search
• Search functionality is a critical component of this platform and will be developed to support
the needs of business customers who typically know the products they need before visiting
the website.
• A full-catalog search bar will be prominently displayed in the header and accessible from
every page on the site.
• As the user types, autocomplete suggestions will appear in a dropdown. Results are
structured in two levels: the first result will be the matching part series, linking to the catalog
or series listing page; individual part number matches follow below, each linking directly to
the corresponding product page.

Quantity-Based Pricing
• The website will support package-quantity-based pricing structures for products.
• Pricing will automatically adjust based on predefined package quantity thresholds
configured for each product.
• The pricing model will support:
◦ Standard package quantity pricing
◦ Multi-tier quantity pricing
◦ Automatic pricing adjustments based on purchasing quantity
• Current pricing tiers are anticipated to include:
◦ 1 Package
◦ 3 Packages
◦ 5 Packages
◦ 10 Packages
• Final pricing rules and product-specific pricing structures will be provided by the Client.
Purchase Order & Net 30 Payment Terms
• The website will support Net 30 purchasing for approved customer accounts.
• Administrative users will be able to designate specific customer accounts as eligible for Net
30 purchasing. Only approved customers will have access to this payment option during
checkout.
• This functionality will include:
◦ Administrative customer approval
◦ Customer-specific Net 30 access
◦ Restricted payment method visibility
◦ Net 30 checkout option for approved accounts
• The Net 30 functionality will be implemented using the ecommerce platform's payment
configuration and customer permission controls.
• The Net 30 payment method will be implemented by repurposing WooCommerce's native
Cash on Delivery (COD) payment option, renamed to Net 30.
• Visibility of this payment method at checkout will be restricted to customer accounts that
have been manually flagged as eligible by an administrator.
• Customers without this flag will not see the Net 30 option.

Product Certifications
• Certifications (including MFR C of C, Material Certs, Process Certs, and Test Reports) are
available as a free digital add-on at the time of purchase only.
• During checkout, customers may opt in to receive certifications for each eligible product.
When opted in, the certification is automatically added to the cart at $0.00 as a
WooCommerce digital product.
• Upon order completion, the certification files are delivered to the customer via email.
• Customers who opted in at the time of purchase may re-download their certifications from
their order history.
• Certification requests for past orders where the customer did not opt in at the time of
purchase are out of scope for this project.

Change Order
Once the PA is approved, any changes to the scope of work, timeline, or associated costs must be
formally documented through a written Change Order. This process ensures transparency, mutual
agreement, and appropriate allocation of resources.
What Triggers a Change Order?
The following scenarios will require a formal Change Order during a custom design project:
• New Page or Layout Requests
◦ Adding extra pages, screens, or design templates that were not included in the
original scope.
◦ Designing alternate versions for other platforms or devices outside the agreed scope.
• Major Revisions or Design Direction Changes
◦ Requesting a new design direction after initial concept approval.
◦ Submitting significant edits that require redesigning core components (e.g.,
navigation, hero layout, brand visuals).
• Exceeding the Revision Limit
◦ If the number of revision rounds exceeds the limit defined in the PA, additional rounds
will be subject to a Change Order.
Change Order Process
• Every Change Order will:
◦ Clearly describe the requested change and its impact.
◦ Specify any adjustments to project fees, milestones, or payment terms.
◦ Require written approval by the client before new work begins.
Important Notes
• Verbal requests or informal communication (e.g., email threads, calls) will not be treated as
approved unless followed by a signed Change Order.
• Work performed outside the original scope without a documented Change Order may result
in delays and additional charges.
• We will not proceed with any new work that deviates from the approved scope without
formal agreement via Change Order.
